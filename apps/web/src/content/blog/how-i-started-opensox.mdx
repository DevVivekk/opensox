i built opensox to solve my own problem of not being able to find open source projects that were suitable for my specific needs.

i had no idea i would work on it full-time. ever.

but one day, at noon on 10th august 2026, i got a twitter DM from a guy named daksh saying "hey i've bought your Opensox Pro but didn't get any email or next steps to do."

i quickly checked my polar dashboard and saw i had indeed made [my first sale](https://x.com/ajeetprssingh/status/1954369610893181135?s=20).

this was the first moment when i gathered some courage to work on my project.

but still not full-time coz i was working at TurboML.

i was planning to work on this project along with my job, but destiny had something else in mind.

my father got a severe slipped disc and couldn't walk properly.

so i had to immediately move back to my village to support my family.

now, whenever i used to get some time, i started working on the project, improved the ui, and added features in case a customer bought opensox pro.

for the next two months, october and november of 2025, i was not able to pick up my laptop.

but still i kept on getting users. people bought opensox pro even when it wasn't even fully completed.

i still remember getting Razorpay msgs while i was waiting outside the Operation Theatre in a hospital in gwalior.

after these two months, my father recovered and started walking properly.

now, i had two choices.

either go back to the job and see the product die slowly.

or

work on a product that looked incredibly uncertain.

given my circumstances, i wanted to choose the first one.

but an inner voice in my heart wasn't ready.

it kept on saying "you could find a job later on but not an opportunity to work on something meaningful at this age"

it was an incredibly scary decision but i chose the second one. thinking "will see what happens next"

and here i am today. still working on it. still going on.
