so far, i've got around 13 offers (the ones i joined + the ones i didn't).

11 of them came from my connections and online presence.

sharing everything i learnt about building a vibrant online presence that kept me employed.

start from the basics:

building an online presence isn't "optional". if you still have the notion, change it. you will know why in the thread ahead.

also, before i tell you how to start, i wanna share some common positive/negative notions.

1. number of followers != genuine connections.

there are lots of folks who have 10k, 20k followers and still struggle in getting referrals and reaching out.

and it's because they have followers, not genuine connections. genuine connections are the ones who will refer you, listen to you, and help you when you need. followers are just a number.

2. posting more won't fasten audience building.

since the start, ive focused only on sharing the stuff that i built and sharing my own experiences and these things have limits.

if i start posting other things for the sake of posting more, i will lose my genuineness and again, genuine connections won't build.

3. you are always ready.

most common problem my friends tell me while building an online presence is "what to post?" "am not ready?".

that's the catch, you have to start before you are ready.

i started when i didn't even have a single internship in my portfolio and later it helped me land multiple offers.

4. i don't want to sound cringe.

many people take an honour saying "haha, i don't shitpost like you" while being unemployed.

the fact of the market is "if you don't get attention, you don't get anything"

better to be a shitposter making money than a proud unemployed who has no online presence.

now, how to build the audience?

1. do the basics first, have an account (with unique dp - it matters) and a good bio.

if you don't have any idea of these copy the ones (not word to word) who you think have a vibrant audience.

2. what to post?

- you lil wins (prs merged, appreciation from maintainers, streaks, etc)

- your learnings (get a good image for thumbnail and write a catchy blog on that)

- your observations (we all observe lots of good things while studying, working, share those, for many people it could be new and helpful)

- a lil of of your personal life (that's subjective.

i didn't use to do it before but as a socially expressive person, i love to share events of my life and it brings joy.

also i got many real friends and connections from this as our background/home town/memories/ideology match.

i love it. it's your choice if you wanna do it or not)

once you posted for 30 days, you will start getting many ideas to post stuff and from there you'll never need to search what to post.

3. DOs while building the audience

- help other builder by commenting or reposting their content.

- while commenting/dming people, ask SPECIFIC questions.

never do "how can i get a 15 lpa job" bullshit.

ask like "my goal is seed and series A startups so how can i target them and what they ask in the interviews, assignment? trial period? projects? or what"

be as specific as you can and see yourself getting answers from really successful people.

i got it from lovable ceo, an usa based investor, mannu paji, archie, just to name a few.
