How I prepared for GSoC'24? (and the mistakes I did)

Part-1: Selecting the right organisation. With links and my notion notes.

## How it started

I heard about GSoC in around September 2023. Before that I have no idea what is open source? How contributions work in open source and how to contribute? Even after that I decided to go for GSoC as it seemed cool and I was also looking for my first internship.

After deciding, I searched on youtube "How to prepare for GSoC" and got few steps to follow. These were as follows.

1. Select the orgs
2. Make contributions
3. When projects announced, make proposal
4. Submit the proposal
5. Keep on making contributions

## 1. Select the Orgs

In order to select the orgs, I went to the [GSoC website](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/programs/2024/organizations) and started searching for orgs that match my tech stack (MERN). But problem was on youtube and all over the internet, there were only general advice "select orgs that match your skills, select orgs that you feel you can contribute to". But no one told How? So I stopped searching on youtube.

After that I followed these steps:

Selected around 15 orgs with tags 'MERN' 'JavaScript', 'TypeScript', 'React' etc (in your case, these tags may be different based on your skills like web3, ai, frontend, etc).

My list was looking like [this](https://ajeetpratapsingh.notion.site/ORG-selection-for-gsoc-24-by-Ajeet-8b084dfdfa22470db7715649ca46c9a5).

## 2. Trimming the list

After this, it was time to trim this list down to 2-3 orgs. To trim down I made a criteria. If an org matches that criteria, only then I'd keep it in the list otherwise remove it. The criteria was:

- Org should not be overcrowded (coz I was first time contributor)
- Org should match my tech stack
- Org has a past of selecting at least 7-10 interns
- Org has good mentors (only know when you join and spend some time)
- Org has clear instructions to setup the project locally (many folks gets stuck on this)
- It should not be the first time org is going for GSoC

**Note:** These were just my thoughts as I had no one to guide me. It was just me and my mind. Please don't copy and apply them as it is in your case. Best way to use it is by taking idea and thought process and design your own strategy.

## 3. Getting started

After selecting the three orgs, I did the following steps:

- Joined their community server (discord, slack etc)
- Introduced myself (TIP: don't say I am here for GSoC even if you are.)
- Picked small issues to fix
- Started fixing issues

After 15-20 days of contributing in these three orgs I realised which org is better for me so I trimmed down to two.

By the end of December, I started getting pace and fixing complex problems. But I was getting unproductive by switching between the different codebases so I had to take a decision to either make solid contributions in one or make average contributions in two. I chose the first one.

And that got me a paid remote internship.
