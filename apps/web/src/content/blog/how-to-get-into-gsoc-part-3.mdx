How I prepared for GSoC'24? (and the mistakes I did)

Part-3: Writing a solid proposal. (with demo and my proposal)

As I told in [part-1](/blog/how-to-get-into-gsoc-part-1) and [part-2](/blog/how-to-get-into-gsoc-part-2) of this thread, solid contributions is the single most important factor for the selection in GSoC and other GSoC like programmes. But in many cases a solid proposal also plays a crucial role for the selection. I'll tell you why? and how?

Here are things that make a solid proposal

1. Include the PR's and issues you've raised in the past in the org's repo. Most of the mentors prefer a contributor who has made PR's than the one who has not. Also not just keep on making PR's, raise issues and suggest new ideas to improve the software. It's just my opinion that you should target to make around 15-20 quality code PR's and get them merged.

Again it's just an opinion, I have seen people getting selected with only 1 or 2 PR's even with none. But why not make a great goal?

2. Write detailed implementation plan. Even if you haven't done such project before. Google is yours. Do extensive research and make a detailed implementation plan that clearly demonstrates how you will implement the project.

How to write detailed implementation plan?

In a proposal there is simply two parts, WHAT and HOW.

first write WHAT you have to do in order to implement the project then write HOW you implement them. (again if you don't know what and how, research) Also divide the WHAT and HOW in their individual pieces. Here is an example.

![WHAT example](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/GT8h1-Jb0AEZ8yM?format=jpg&name=large)

Once you have decided WHAT you need to do to complete the project end to end, move on to the HOW part.

For each point that you have written in WHAT section. write HOW you'll implement it. if you don't know, again, research.

Here is an example :

![HOW example](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/GT8h3JIb0AUH-dL?format=jpg&name=medium)

(Fun Fact: My proposal was 40 pages long)

3. You'll hear many times that make all the three proposals. But in contrast, I believe in making one great proposal than making 3 average one's. Also I have heard from one of the mentors of a GSoC org that folks who make all the three proposals in the same org, the org don't take them seriously (might be wrong). Though it's just my opinion. Someone may be truly exceptional enough to make all the three proposals great.

4. I'll share my proposal in our discord community. I added the link in the end of this thread.

SOME IMPORTANT TIPS

- finish writing your proposal early (atleast 1 week before the deadline) so that you can take feedback from the mentors and improve it.

- don't ask mentors "how to make proposal?" It's a sure shot way to get rejected. make your proposal first and then ask for feedback. (Remember it's not our school where the one who asks lots of questions, becomes class monitor)

- add code blocks, demos, diagrams and other stuff in the proposal. (Humans are visual creature)

- get your proposal reviewed from your seniors or someone who did GSoC like stuff before. ( Unfortunately, i had no one to review). (You can follow me. I'll review some of the proposals for gsoc25 haha)

- keep it simple and minimal. Don't add any fancy designs, typography or Motu Patlu logo. just raw and effective content.
