i had 3 yrs to save my life.

in 2023, i was stuck in a weird dilemma.

i was applying to thousands of jobs and internships but none of them were hiring (not even replying).

coz i had no experience (yes, most ask exp for internships too), no good college tag, and no connections — and because i was not getting hired, i had no experience.

every single day. every single "unfortunately, we've decided..", every single "hi sir, im ajeet.." were getting me closer to depression and mental trauma.

just for context, this time i was 20 yo. i had left a hotel job that i joined in 2021 and was in my village with my family.

every day, i had two tasks.

doing household farming to help my father (he never asked but i felt shame if i didn't) and somehow finding time to learn tech so that i could escape my default fate of being a mediocre farmer in UP.

and i had 3 yrs to do it.

of that, i had spent 2022 to mid 2023 learning html and css and collecting certificates, thinking someone would offer me a job for these.

so in this time, i was throwing my hands and feet here and there, like a kid thrown into water trying to survive, just to get some exposure, experience and connections so that at least i could get started and get some work to do.

i tried -

- submitting thousands of applications on linkedin
- DMing founders and CTOs
- pinging ppl on twitter to work on their side projects for free
- calling my hotel owner and his friends to ask if they needed a website
- calling friends who were a bit well-known at bigger colleges to get some help
- and so on

nothing worked.

at this point i started thinking that if nothing worked in these 2 yrs, i'd go back to my hotel job and try again after that.

but soon i realized the problem is that im just a raw. blank. nobody.

people reject me coz people don't see any value in me.

they don't believe i can produce something meaningful for them.

so to fix this, all i needed was my first opportunity, so that i could remove this curse of blankness.

one day i was watching a tutorial on youtube and it recommended a video on open source to me.

it was someone explaining what it is and how it works.

i didn't understand it properly but i got a sense that here they don't ask for a resume or experience, so probably i should try.

i understood more about open source and then realized that it literally doesn't require any prerequisites but just the work.

for the first time in many weeks, i was really happy this time.

i watched more videos on it and started learning about it.

learnt git and github. learnt about terms like pull request, merge, review, local setup, origin, upstream, etc

everything was going fine but soon i realized contributing to open source isn't as easy as i thought initially.

in DSA, you have some fundamental patterns and problem solving techniques that you can learn over a period of time and get good at it.

but in open source, things keep on changing. because it involves humans.

a community that was active before may have closed.

an issue that you were working on can be assigned to someone else.

your PR can be sidelined for no reason.

and the worst part, as a lone hustler (like me), you have no one to ask why.

in most cases, you doubt your skills and sometimes you accept the fate.

when i started open source, the first 6-7 months were absolutely soul-crushing.

the feeling of no one reacting to your "hi, im ajeet. im here to contribute" intro msgs.

the feeling of not getting any attention in the community even though i was interacting.

the feeling of not getting replies to my comments under the issues.

the feeling of not getting any issue assigned.

the feeling of working on a PR for 4 weeks and then not getting even a single look from the maintainer, or worse, hearing "we don't need it as of now" from the maintainer.

the feeling of being sidelined coz someone else is more talented than you.

but i had no choice but to go through it and win.

because if i didn't, my whole world would be limited to my village.

i would become a frog in a well.

i would see my potential getting wasted second by second like a life sentence.

i would become a mediocre farmer working 24 * 7 * 365 just to put enough food on the table to survive the next 6 months.

and i would never choose it.

first 6-7 months in open source were like hell for me, i was learning through trial and error.

got mocked for stupid questions.

got scolded for bad implementation.

got sidelined coz of being less confident. and what not.

i was doing everything and anything this time.

except one thing. giving up.

BUT slowly and gradually, i started noticing very tiny growth.

a maintainer liked my comment. got a reply in the community. someone commented "will take a look" on my pull request.

if you tell these things to someone in tech it'll sound stupid.

but to me it was a moment that changed my fate. it was the moment that pumped more blood in my veins to be more hyper-active.

because only this time, i saw a thin string of light to end the curse of being blank.

and i was RIGHT. as i kept going for a long time.

things changed.

my first pull request got merged (i had tears in my eyes at that moment)

my first 10 pull requests got merged

got the first paid internship

bought a good laptop with that money (m2 air)

got a second internship

a third..

job interviews at lovable, blinkit, Tower Research, etc

got 10k+ followers on twitter

built connections with people i had never even imagined. CEOs, CTOs, maintainers, Engineers, etc.

and most importantly, i was employable. i could get out of my home.

and i did.

but all of this took me around 3 years, insane hard work, countless embarrassing mistakes, many depressed weeks, and the worst mental health.

all of this coz i was doing it through trial and error. no seniors. no friends. no connections. no nothing.

just intuition, actions and results.
