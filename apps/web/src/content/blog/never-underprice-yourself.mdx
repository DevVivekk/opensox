your habit of desperately underpricing your work/product/service can kill your future faster than lack of skills or opportunities.

when i was desperate for my first internship, it was very tempting to do an unpaid internship.

"let's do some work first and then prob ppl will pay me for it"

that's what i was thinking.

but then i saw some of my friends did the same and still struggling to get PAID work.

why?

soon enough ive realized doing the unpaid work is a mediocre spiral.

- you do unpaid work
- ppl see low/zero price tag
- ppl assume inferior work/product/service
- you don't get to work on smth worthy.
- all u do is chores.
- now you don't have any impressive thing in ur portfolio
- so you do unpaid work.

this made me realize that underpricing my work just to get an internship isn't worth my time and efforts.

so i decided a price. for my work.

and that was 15k INR. a month.

it was clear that i won't do any work in less than 15k INR.

no matter how urgent or desperate i feel.

soon enough ive realized my current skills and experience isn't worth 15k and i gotta LEVEL UP to reach that.

so i did.

i built projects, did open source and built a good online presence.

and after 3 months, i got my first internship.

35,000 INR. a month.

because my SELF-PRICE forced me to LEVEL UP.

same thing happened with opensox.

initially when i priced it $49, no one was buying it.

i was stressed. tensed and desperate to get sales.

but i didn't underprice the product.

i worked extremely hard to increase the value for the pro members.

i added an onboarding call, 247 support, unlimited QnAs, weekly sessions, contests and a lot of other things.

today not only i get sales but often ppl saying "you're selling it way too cheap"

proof: [https://opensox.ai/testimonials](https://opensox.ai/testimonials)

similarly with sponsors.

since the day ive started taking content seriously, i've been getting lots of sponsors.

but most of them were either product against my values that ill never promote or too underpriced.

all they were offering $5, $10 or max $100.

(again, low price. inferior product)

so for the last 9 months, ive been rejecting all of them.

but yesterday, on june 4th, 2026, ive got my first sponsor.

for more than the amount i had priced for my collab.

(can't share the exact amount but you can buy a good enough phone w it)

(note: sponsor on twitter. not taking any sponsor on yt as of now for the same reason. LEVELING UP)

in all these examples, the pattern is same.

- price your work/product/service above avg.
- level up to reach that.
- reach that.
- re-price it.
- and grind again.

basically go upwards. not downwards.

and it applies to everything. self-worth. internship. job. freelancing. business. content creation. sponsors.

but if you choose to UNDERPRICE it.

you are not reducing the price.

you're reducing your self-worth. you're reducing your perceived value.

just for a quick win.

here you're optimizing for the quick reward rather than long-term value creation.

and yes, very likely, you will win.

BUT you'll JUST "win".

and with this quick win, you've entered yourself into the spiral of "cheap work/product/service".

now you'll have no pressure to do great work / create the best product / provide the best service.

so your work/service/product will remain inferior.

hence, you won't be able to price it more.

welcome to the mediocre loop.

NOTE:

there're some exceptions and differences.

working on an exciting idea with your friends and not caring too much about the money part is one of the exceptions to this.

because

the potential to build smth big and worthy >>>> current value of ur efforts.

having some urgent personal circumstances is also an exception.

also, things like offering a trial period to a company, offering a free landing page revamp to a possible client, providing free tier to users or demo of your service are different.

things like these are used to let ppl know the true VALUE of your work/product/service so that you can charge them the PRICE YOU feel worthy.

here we're not underpricing.

we're using the free thing as marketing or funnel.

some of those who will get a taste of our work/product/service will pay the price of our work/product/service that we deserve.
