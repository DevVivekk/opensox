title: "Scale Is the Problem"
date: "2026-06-01"
description: "Why scaling a community from family to user base costs authenticity and the genuine vibe."
author: "Ajeet"
tag: "misc"
---

have you ever noticed that something that started as a really nice small community or a small project got its vibe destroyed when that community or project was scaled to millions of people?

it's not a coincidence. it's simple economics.

when there are fewer people in a community or project.

we treat it like our family. we enjoy the process. we love working together. and we can spend time with each other.

but when a founder decides to "scale" it to hundreds of thousands or millions of people, that "family" becomes a "user base".

now it's not humanly possible to spend time with the users, talk to them, and engage with them.

the cost of scale is authenticity and the genuine vibe.

now that community or project becomes more like a number-obsessed machine and less like a true neighborhood community of people with the same interests and goals.

that's why sometimes scale is the problem.
