when i was 14, i wanted to be something way bigger than elon musk.

i wanted to build startups with billions of users around the world.

i wanted to build companies worth billions.

i wanted to be "Big" or even "The Biggest".

when i started working on opensox, i wanted to scale it like crazy.

i wanted to raise millions of VC money.

i wanted the title of "youngest billionaire ever".

BUT

soon i realized [why scale is a problem](/blog/scale-is-the-problem)

and i was at a point where i had to make a trade-off.

it was—

do i want to make a small impact on millions of lives, or a life-changing impact on a few hundred?

the cost of choosing the first was my authenticity and the cost of choosing the second was all the dreams i had since i was 14.

took me some time to realize i will not think about the numbers when it'll be my last 10 minutes on earth.

i'll probably think about the love i got from a few people, the time i spent with friends and family, and the people i've helped.

this realization erased my definition of a successful life and set up a new one.

so it became clear. i will prefer to create life-changing impact on a few hundred people than a slight impact on millions.

hence, i stay small.
