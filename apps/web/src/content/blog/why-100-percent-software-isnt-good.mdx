what if i scrap all the open source projects, filter them and then manually labels them like "this oss project hires contributors", "this project is active", "this project has great maintainers".

so that a new open source contributor doesn't have to waste weeks of his/her time to find a suitable open source project to contribute.

that was the BIG IDEA behind Opensox.

and i started with a very [small and crappy version](https://x.com/jackedAJ/status/1871152195120558189?s=20) of this idea.

people liked it.

i got something very early that most startups struggle with for a long time.

VALIDATION.

my idea was validated. i knew the problem exists and this could be a possible solution.

when i took a break in september 2025, i had around 1.5 lakhs in my pocket.

saved from my previous internships and work.

out of which i spent around 90k on [my father's surgery](https://x.com/jackedAJ/status/1977307880446697493).

and i was left with 60k.

i estimated everything. and realized.

60k INR was the enough money to build this BIG IDEA.

and some money will be left (~10k) for the worst scenario so that i could get a job if things didn't go well (most likely)

as it would take time so ive [hired an intern](https://x.com/jackedAJ/status/1992576588295307444).

DM'd some friends to ask for technical suggestions.

planned the [high level architecture](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1aJ0NsQy-w-B9MsJGIBWYDHcZia57_Thi/view?usp=sharing).

and broke down the BIG IDEA into smaller weekly tasks.

BUT wait....

let's say i build a search feature like this - scrap the oss projects, filter and manually label them with the useful tags.

but how am i going to track those tags later on?

i mean let's say i tag a project "helpful maintainers" then how will i know if maintainers are still "helpful" in that OSS project.

coz people change. policies change. companies change. market change. and so on.

how am i going to keep those tags accurate all the time?

you can say just check the project again as you did before. manually.

yes. but for millions of projects?? every week?? manually?

i was stuck at this point and was finding the answer.

as i didn't want to raise any VC money for my idea and didn't have a big fund in my savings, trying a highly expensive, complex and big system didn't seem a good way.

and even if i did it, would it be helpful to the end user?

would it be helpful to that desperate ajeet who wanted to contribute to open source because he wasn't getting an internship 4 yrs ago?

not sure. not even today.

then slowly ive realized probably 100% software isn't a solution.

because the problems you face while trying to make a place in tech through open source require a real human at many occasions.

and that's why <a href="/blog/stay-small" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">staying small</a> seemed the most suitable direction.

here in [opensox pro](https://opensox.ai/pricing), this problem is solved as i provide hand-picked open source projects so that you can contribute from the day one.
