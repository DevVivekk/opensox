"if i open source, someone will copy my product"

i had this notion at one point of time. but open sourcing my product (http://opensox.ai) changed the world for me.

how? 🧵

first of all understand why open-sourcing a project won't make your idea get copied.

1. do you really think you have a unique idea? come one man! ideas are cheap. everyone's getting the ideas you have. ideas are never the moat.

2. execution is all that matters. and if i have to be specific - iterations (experiments, execution and validated learning) is the key.

3. "but they'll copy the code of my products awesome features?" products' on! code can always be copied, even of proprietary software. microsoft created msexcel so did google.

4. the real differentiator is DISTRIBUTION AND VIBE. Distribution is simple - if you have 100 people that you can reach up to anytime and they'd listen, you have a distribution of 100 ppl. and vibe is basically the taste of a product - it includes everything, from what kind of fonts we use to how we reply to emails to how customers perceive you. vibe is all about creating a (genuine and authentic) perception.

5. creating a DISTRIBUTION and VIBE takes time. there's not chatGPT for it.

6. someone can copy the code, but never the distribution and vibe you've created in those years.

so now why should you open source ur product?

1. when you open source, you start getting attention (from contributors, communities, and even companies), and "that's all you need" 😉. literally, you'll be amazed to see how many people come forward to help you.

2. when you open source, "more eyes" theory kicks in. out of nowhere, someone finds bugs in ur project, someone's improving ui, someone's writing ci/cd pipelines, someone's suggesting new ideas, and someone senior is giving your advice on how to scale it - all that for free (coz of community)

3. another benefit is that you get a faster feedback loop. for proprietary software, they only get feedback from their users but open source products get feedback from everyone - contributors, users, community, maintainers, etc. there (in proprietary software, you'll have to work hard to get the feedback, and here (open source products), it will automatically come to you.

4. by open sourcing, you build community. community brings trust. trust brings you a useful product, and that brings revenue and value of everyone.

5. your work becomes public - everyone sees it - ceos, ctos, future investors or future wife. just sayin. who knows.

6. also a fact: open source companies are more likely to get funding in 2025

7. hiring becomes too easy. how? just hire the top contributors of the project.

8. marketing becomes easy too. how? more contributors and more community means more word of mouth. there's no reason i won't suggest a software to someone in which i have contributed to.

9. running an open source software is super cheap. many companies offer free pro plans for open source projects like vercel, coderabit, github, etc

10. sometimes users fix their own problems. (but you don't have to get lazy.)

11. green carpet streak

12. in the worst case, if the project fails, you still have gigachad work portfolio to land a job with handsome salary.

finally, ive open source http://opensox.ai 3 months ago and it's the best decision so far.
