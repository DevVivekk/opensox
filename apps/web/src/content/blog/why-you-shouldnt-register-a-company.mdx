if you are planning to build a revenue-first business (without any external funding), only register your company when you cross 2-3 lakhs in net profits.

why?

1. initially, ur idea isn't validated so you don't know whether it will even work on not. you may need to pivot and it's way easier when ur company is small and not registered

2. as you are relying on your own profits, registering it very early will cost you money and time.

and both of these things are the lifeline for your business. you can't afford to waste it.

3. it's not even important. just get a working product and focus of getting validation by building and experimenting fast.

4. once you have the validation and a decent enough product, money will flow and you can easily delegate these tasks to someone else.

5. core idea: if it's not important for your objectives (you must have an objective. mine is 5k MRR), don't do it.
